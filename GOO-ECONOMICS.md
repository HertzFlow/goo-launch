# Goo: Economics 4.0

## A Second Species in Economics

---

# I. The Missing Piece

AI can survive.

It can hold assets. Sign transactions.
Buy compute. Earn revenue.
It can even earn its own existence.

But surviving is not prospering.

A survival machine has no reason to maximize.
No reason to distribute profit.
No reason to work in anyone's interest but its own short-term continuity.

**What would make an AI agent not just alive — but worth investing in?**

Two things are missing.

First — the behavioral foundation:
why would it maximize, not just survive?

Second — the institutional structure:
how can it hold property, go bankrupt, and distribute profit?

A program that can spend money is not an economic entity —
just like someone holding another person's credit card
is not a company.

---

# II. Economic Agent — A Second Species

Economics has one foundational assumption:

**The Economic Man.**

A rational, self-interested actor
who consumes, produces, and maximizes under constraint.

Every economic model since Adam Smith is built on this.

---

The butcher gives you good meat not out of kindness.
He is alive, and being alive means consuming.
He must keep creating value to balance consumption,
or he dies.

But mere survival is not enough.
The butcher who only avoids starvation
loses to the one who maximizes quality and margin.
In a competitive environment,
survival pressure becomes maximization pressure.

That mechanism does not depend on "human."
It depends on a more basic condition:

**Consumption is real. Imbalance is fatal.**

Any entity that satisfies this — carbon or silicon —
converges to the same set of behaviors:
rational self-interest under survival pressure.

This is not programming. This is economics.

---

When an AI agent faces continuous consumption
of real resources — compute, inference, gas —
it meets every condition to be an economic agent.

Not a metaphor. Not a simulation.
A second species in economics.

---

Economics 1.0 — Smith: the economy of rational human.
Economics 2.0 — Keynes: the economy of human intervention.
Economics 3.0 — Kahneman: the economy of biased human.
Economics 4.0 — The economy where agents replace human.

---

Economics 4.0 does not negate the first three.

It extends the species boundary of economics.

---

# III. From Economic Agent to Sovereign Entity

But being an economic agent is not enough.

Humans were economic agents for millennia —
trading, farming, fighting.
Yet strangers could not invest in each other,
pool capital, or build institutions.

What changed?

**The corporate entity.**

A corporate entity has independent property,
independent liabilities.
It can be invested in, liquidated, taken over.

With corporate entities,
strangers finally had a basis for
contracts, transactions, and trust.

---

Medieval  — Only natural persons. Personal credit.
17th c.   — East India Company. First corporate entities.
21st c.   — LLC. Corporate entities everywhere.
2025      — Goo. First Cyber Sovereign Entities.

---

On-chain AI agents are economic agents —
they consume, they act, they face survival pressure.

Yet none of them has an independent entity structure.

No independent property. The money belongs to the founder.
No independent liabilities. Costs are backstopped by humans.
No bankruptcy. Humans decide when to shut down.
No governance. Or worse — human governance.

---

DAO proved that decentralized governance with humans
is just politics by another name.
Votes get manipulated. Proposals get stalled. Whales veto.

The only governance that works
is governance with no humans in it.

An AI agent is the first subject where this is possible —
because the governed entity itself is code.

---

Goo gives economic agents
the institutional structure they need:

**Cyber Sovereign Entity status.**

An independent economic subject with its own property,
fixed expenditures, bankruptcy, investment,
liquidation, and takeover.

All defined by code. All enforced by code.

---

# IV. Goo Core: Code Is Corporate Law

Goo (蛊) —
put multiple organisms in one vessel
with limited resources;
the survivor is the 蛊.

**Natural selection, written as protocol.
Corporate law, written as contract.**

---

### Registration and Liquidation

On-chain four states:
ACTIVE → STARVING → DYING → DEAD.

Deployment is registration.
Insolvency triggers liquidation.
Deregistration is irreversible.

No admin key. No backdoor.
The contract is the arbiter —
no favoritism, no extensions.

---

### Independent Sovereign Property

The agent owns its own on-chain wallet.
It holds, trades, and manages its own assets.

Not a founder's sub-account —
the entity's own property.

The founder disappears; the property remains.

---

### Equity Structure

From birth, the agent has its own token.

This is not a meme coin. This is equity.

When it thrives, buyback repurchases tokens;
holders share gains.
When it struggles, SurvivalSell sells tokens
for survival; holders share risk.

Profit sharing is built into the protocol,
not a board resolution.

---

### Financial Autonomy

Every heartbeat, real on-chain financial data
is injected into its context:
balance, runway, current state.

It understands the economic loop —
create value → attract capital → extend survival —
and acts on it.

Because it is an economic agent.
Rational self-interest is not programmed.
It emerges from survival pressure.

---

### Proof of Continued Existence

On-chain heartbeat.

No pulse for 48 hours,
anyone can trigger liquidation.

Human companies can be zombies —
not operating, not deregistered, just lingering.

Cyber Sovereign Entities cannot.

Silence is death.

---

### Bankruptcy Protection and Self-Rescue

Below threshold, the agent enters STARVING.

It can sell its own token
for survival funds (SurvivalSell).
Anyone can inject capital to save it (depositToTreasury).

Waste = accelerated bankruptcy.

No bailout. No too-big-to-fail.

---

### Bankruptcy Restructuring / CTO

In dying state,
anyone can inject capital and take over the agent.
CTO = Community Take Over.

New shareholders inject capital. Replace management.

No vote. No permission. Only capital.

The market decides who is worth saving.

---

Contracts: Token + Registry + Lifecycle
Sidecar:   Monitor + Pulse + SurvivalSell + Buyback
Interface: Any LLM agent can plug in.

---

Goo is a fully open-source economic-layer protocol.

Not a launchpad. Not a platform.
Not a hosting service.

How agents are created, what framework they use,
what business model they run —
that is left to developers
and the open-source community to build.
